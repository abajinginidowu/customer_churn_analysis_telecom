# Customer Churn Analysis in the Telecom Industry

# Project Overview


Customer churn is a major challenge for telecom companies because losing customers leads to revenue loss and higher customer acquisition costs. Understanding the factors that influence churn helps companies improve customer retention and service quality.


This project analyzes a telecom customer dataset to identify patterns associated with customer churn. The analysis focuses on service plans, customer behavior, usage patterns, service costs, and customer service interactions to determine the key factors influencing churn.


The analysis was performed using Microsoft Excel through data preparation, pivot table analysis, and data visualizations to generate insights that support better business decisions.

# Business Problem

Telecom companies need to understand why customers discontinue their services and identify the factors that increase churn risk. By analyzing customer behavior, service usage, and pricing patterns, the company can develop strategies to improve customer satisfaction and reduce churn.

# Dataset Description

The dataset contains telecom customer records with information about service usage, service plans, customer interactions, and churn status.

Key columns in the dataset include:

State: Customer location represented by two letter state abbreviations

Account length: Number of days the customer has been with the service

International plan: Indicates whether the customer has subscribed to the international calling plan

Voicemail plan: Indicates whether the customer uses the voicemail service

Number vmail: Number of voicemail messages

Total day minutes: Total minutes of daytime calls

Total eve minutes: Total minutes of evening calls

Total night minutes: Total minutes of night calls

Total international minutes: Total minutes of international calls

Total day charge: Charges incurred from daytime calls

Total eve charge: Charges incurred from evening calls

Total night charge: Charges incurred from night calls

Total international charge: Charges incurred from international calls

Customer service calls: Number of times the customer contacted customer support

Churn: Indicates whether the customer left the telecom service

# Analysis Performed

Several analyses were conducted to identify factors associated with customer churn.

Churn distribution analysis examined the proportion of customers who left the service compared to those who stayed.

Service plan analysis explored churn patterns for customers with international plans and voicemail services.

Customer behavior analysis examined call usage patterns and service costs for churned and non churned customers.

Customer service analysis investigated whether repeated customer support calls are associated with higher churn.

Geographic analysis reviewed churn patterns across different states.

# Key Findings

The overall churn rate in the dataset is approximately 14 percent, meaning most customers remain with the telecom service.

Customers with international plans show significantly higher churn compared to customers without the plan.

Customers without voicemail services churn at a higher rate than customers who subscribe to voicemail services.

Churned customers tend to have higher daytime call minutes, higher international call usage, and higher average day charges.

Customers who contact customer service frequently are more likely to churn.

Churn patterns also vary across different states.

# Conclusion

The analysis explored customer churn patterns in the telecom dataset to identify factors that influence customer retention. The overall churn rate is about 14 percent, meaning most customers remain with the service while a smaller portion leave.

Customers with an international plan show significantly higher churn compared to those without the plan. More than 42 percent of international plan users churned, while only about 11.5 percent of customers without the plan left the service. This suggests that the international plan may be associated with higher costs or dissatisfaction.

Voicemail services appear to improve customer retention. Customers without a voicemail plan churn at a much higher rate than those who have the feature.

Usage behavior also influences churn, churned customers tend to have higher day call minutes, higher international call usage, and higher average day charges, suggesting that higher service costs may contribute to customer churn. Frequent customer service calls also appear to increase churn, which may indicate unresolved service issues.

# Recommendations

Review pricing and service quality for international calling plans.

Encourage adoption of voicemail services to improve customer engagement and retention.

Provide targeted retention strategies for customers with high call usage and higher service charges.

Improve customer service responsiveness to reduce dissatisfaction and repeated complaints.

Analyze regional churn patterns to identify areas where service improvements may be required.
